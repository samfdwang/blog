An Inequality proved by Jensen Inequality

Sep 18, 2017 Inequality Inequality

Statement of the problem: suppose $x\in \mathbb{R}^n$ and $\sum_{i=1}^nx_i=0$, show that $$ \text{max} x_j>\frac{|x|_2}{n} $$ My proof is as following. After reordering and relabeling the $x_i$, we can finally have:

$$x_1+\cdots+xk=x{k+1}+\cdots+x_n$$

where left side consists of positive numbers while the right side consists of non-positive numbers. Then we have $$ \begin{split} |x|_2^2 &= x_1^2+\cdots+x_n^2
&\leq x_1^2+\cdots+xk^2+(x{k+1}+\cdots+x_n)^2
&=x_1^2+\cdots+x_k^2+(x_1+\cdots+x_k)^2
&\leq x_1^2+\cdots+x_k^2+k(x_1^2+\cdots+x_k^2)
&=(1+k)(x_1^2+\cdots+x_k^2)
\end{split} $$ Hence $$ \frac{|x|_2}{n}\leq \frac{(1+k)^{1⁄2}|\bar{x}|_2}{n}\leq \frac{\sqrt{(1+k)k}x^}{n}<x^ $$ where $\bar{x}=(x_1,x_2,…,x_k)$ and $x^*$ stands for maximum of $x$, and here we have used $k(k+1)<n$ for all $k<n,k,n\in \mathbb{N}$
